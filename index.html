About Andy

Andy Warhol was an American artist, director and producer who was a leading figure in the visual art movement known as pop art. His works explore the relationship between artistic expression, celebrity culture, and advertising that flourished by the 1960s, and span a variety of media, including painting, silkscreening, photography, film, and sculpture.

  • “If you're not trying to be real, you don't have to get it right. That's art.”

    — Andy Warhol

  • “They always say time changes things, but you actually have to change them yourself.”

    — Andy Warhol

  • “You have to do stuff that average people don't understand because those are the only good things.”

  • “A picture means I know where I was every minute. That's why I take pictures. It's a visual diary.”

Statue of Liberty

Double Marlon

$32,521,000

Signed and dated twice 'Andy Warhol 1966' (on the overlap) silkscreen ink on unprimed linen.

Silver Liz

$28,165,000

Signed and dated 'Andy Warhol 65' (on the overlap of the left panel) spray enamel, synthetic polymer and silkscreen inks on canvas.

Four Marilyns

$36,005,000

The Four Marilyns is a work of art produced in 1964 by the artist. It consists of four canvases, each a square measuring 40 inches and each consisting of a painting of a Marilyn Monroe, each shot through in the forehead by a single bullet.
